Childhood

I was born and grew up in Jodhpur, Rajasthan. Early years were full of vivid moments playing tennis, diving into computer games like GTA and Need for Speed, and listening to music or playing cricket. Even back then, I dreamt of becoming an astrophysicist to uncover how the Universe works. Tennis and hanging out with friends were my constant companions, and those experiences quietly shaped both my curiosity and my competitive spirit.

School years

I attended St. Paul’s Sr. Sec. School in Jodhpur, where I loved science, maths, and history. School life balanced academics with athletics: I played tennis competitively up to the national level and won several trophies, turning a hobby into a serious pursuit. Free time was mostly about enjoying moments with friends.

I also faced a significant academic bump between grades 6–9, when mathematics was hard to grasp for me. That challenge felt tough in the moment, but getting through it taught me resilience and patience, skills that would matter far beyond those years.

College and higher studies

For my undergraduate studies, I chose to study BSc (Hons) Physics at Amity University, Noida. Early years of university were extremely rewarding for me as I was finally studying the subject I always loved--Physics. During this time I was formally introduced to Astrophysics and I instantly fell in love. Then I moved to Germany to pursue my MSc in Astrophysics from the University of Bonn. These decisions were a natural step towards the dream I had since childhood. The hardest and most meaningful coursework came in cosmology and theoretical astrophysics during my master’s which was extremely rigorous, but deeply rewarding. I learned to wrestle with complex ideas and high standards, which pushed me to grow both intellectually and personally.

Collaboration became a central part of this stage. I co‑authored two research papers, and one of them was a lead‑author paper based on my master’s thesis. Seeing that work come together was a proud milestone. Through these years I discovered something vital about myself: I was actually good at what I did, and that realization changed how I viewed my goals and capabilities.

Current life and interests

I’m now pursuing a PhD in astrophysics at the University of Rome Tor Vergata. My research builds on galaxy clusters and their observations in X‑ray and sub‑millimeter wavebands. Presenting my work, contributing to multi‑institution efforts, and even making new discoveries have been part of the journey. Each one taking me back to my childhood fascination with the Universe.

Day to day, I stay motivated by both the big questions and the practical steps forward: analyzing data, learning new methods, and working alongside peers and mentors who challenge me. The blend of persistence, curiosity, and teamwork that began in Jodhpur continues to guide me in Rome.

Looking ahead

Looking forward, my goal is to keep pushing the boundaries of what we know about galaxy clusters, dark matter, and the large‑scale structure of the universe. The path from playing games in Jodhpur to doing cutting‑edge research today shows how curiosity, even when it feels impossible, can become a lifelong mission. Each challenge along the way is a chance to learn, adapt, and move closer to understanding the cosmos a little better.
